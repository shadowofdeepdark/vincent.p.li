Current projects and ongoing investigations

Current Projects

Parafoveal Effects of Emojis in Sarcasm Comprehension

Dr Patrick Sturt Carnegie Trust Scholarship

Examining how emojis in parafoveal vision influence the processing of sarcastic statements during Chinese text reading. Using boundary-paradigm eye-tracking to measure real-time integration of visual and linguistic cues.

Eye-tracking Multimodal Chinese Boundary Paradigm
Status: Data collection complete (n=55), preliminary analyses show parafoveal emoji effects

Quotation Framing and Sarcasm Comprehension

Professor Martin Corley Pre-registered

Investigating whether direct quotations (he said "this is great") facilitate sarcasm comprehension compared to indirect quotations (he said that it was great). Testing how narrative perspective influences pragmatic interpretation.

Self-paced Reading Sarcasm Pragmatics
Status: Data collection ongoing, preliminary mixed-effects analyses underway

Communicative Context and Language Prediction

Professor Martin Corley

Examining whether explicitly communicative contexts shift language predictions from typicality-driven to informativity-driven expectations, measured through visual attention patterns.

Eye-tracking Visual World Prediction
Status: Currently collecting data using EyeLink 1000 and OpenSesame

Prediction Under Adverse Conditions

Professor Martin Pickering's HollyMartin Lab ESRC-funded

Multi-site collaboration investigating how cognitive load, noise, and hearing impairment affect prediction mechanisms in language comprehension. Coordinated Edinburgh site data collection.
